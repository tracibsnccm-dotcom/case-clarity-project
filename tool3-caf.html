Before You Begin — Understanding the C.A.R.E. Access Friction Score™

Twenty-five questions across five domains. The report highlights systemic barriers to access and continuity—not individual blame. Goal: clarity, not speed.

Disruptions often come from payer, authorization, logistics, and documentation friction—not from a single person. This tool helps you spot those barriers early for monitoring and file documentation.

  • Answer from the case environment as it stands today.
  • Take enough time to be accurate; rushed answers skew the picture.
  • Reconcile C.A.R.E. (Clinical Analysis & Recovery Entitlements) is the broader methodology; this score is one structured lens on access friction.

Each domain maps to common failure points in treatment access. The lowest domain level drives the overall friction level.

  • 1. Provider Access — availability, referrals, willingness to treat in litigation/lien contexts.
  • 2. Insurance & authorization — payer rules, UR, delays, denials.
  • 3. Transportation & travel — getting to appointments reliably.
  • 4. Administrative & documentation — IDs, records, billing, timeliness.
  • 5. Care coordination — communication across providers and the plan of care.

Functional stability is expressed in whole-number levels, not decimals—small fractional differences do not change the interpreted level. Capability at one level versus the next can differ materially; the assessment avoids false precision.

  • Higher levels are not always achievable (e.g., MMI below “full” function applies to environments too).
  • The aim is an accurate stability picture for decisions—not inflating the score.

Each domain uses its five answers only. Domain totals convert to a whole-number level by rounding down.

The overall Access Friction Score™ follows the lowest domain—the weakest barrier caps system stability.

Barriers can look like non-compliance or poor progress later if they stay invisible. Use this to monitor the file, tighten focus, and document systemic friction while treatment is ongoing. Reconcile C.A.R.E. is built to track and address these same issues across the life of a case.
