How to Maximize Your Investment

To get the most value from Case Clarity Project, we recommend following a structured assessment schedule. Each instrument provides critical insights at different stages of case development, and running them at the right frequency ensures you capture important trajectory changes when they matter most.

Recommended Follow-Up Schedule

Tool Description Minimum Frequency Best Practice
CCI (Case Complexity Index) Assesses case complexity level and identifies key drivers Level 1–2: Minimum every 7 days
Level 3: Minimum every 30 days
Level 4: Minimum every 45 days
Level 5: Minimum every 60 days		 Higher complexity requires tighter monitoring. Catching complexity shifts early prevents case drift.
CSA (Clinical Sequencing Auditor) Evaluates clinical sequencing and identifies gaps in care coordination Minimum every 30 days Most effective when performed AFTER: a provider is added or removed, a treatment request is submitted, or any significant change occurs that may alter the trajectory of treatment.
CAF (C.A.R.E. Access Friction Score) Measures barriers to care and treatment access friction points Minimum every 30 days Most effective when performed AFTER: a provider is added or removed, a treatment request is submitted, or any significant change occurs that may alter the trajectory of treatment.
HSI (Holistic Stability Index) Assesses overall case stability across intrinsic and extrinsic factors Minimum every 30 days Most effective when performed AFTER: a provider is added or removed, a treatment request is submitted, or any significant change occurs that may alter the trajectory of treatment.
FCI (Functional Capacity Index) Evaluates functional capacity and vulnerability indicators Minimum every 30 days Most effective when performed AFTER: a provider is added or removed, a treatment request is submitted, or any significant change occurs that may alter the trajectory of treatment.
RRF (Recovery Risk Forecaster) Predicts recovery trajectory and identifies risk factors Minimum every 30 days Most effective when performed AFTER: a provider is added or removed, a treatment request is submitted, or any significant change occurs that may alter the trajectory of treatment.

Important Context:

While the minimum is every 30 days, it should be expected that these reports would—and should—be run often during the early phases of injury, treatment, and care. Early and frequent assessments capture the trajectory of decline and recovery when it matters most.

You have to run them often enough when the client is at their most vulnerable so that you can catch declines in condition before they undermine the case and the evidence you need to prove how hindered the client was from returning to baseline physically and financially.
