About the Assessment

Who conducts
this assessment.

This assessment is provided by Reconcile Care Management Services, a clinical case management firm specializing in complex injury and disability matters.

Our work focuses on forensic care management, helping attorneys understand the medical, functional, and social factors that influence case outcomes.

The assessment is conducted by a Board Certified Case Manager (CCM) with nursing credentials and experience supporting personal injury, workers' compensation, and disability litigation.

The goal of the Case Clarity Project is simple: to allow attorneys to evaluate the analytical tools directly against their own cases.

The Forensic Tool Suite

Six instruments.
One comprehensive view.

The assessment is performed using six forensic instruments. Each tool examines a different dimension of case complexity, treatment alignment, access barriers, life stability, functional capacity, and recovery risk.

The Case Clarity Project applies six independent forensic instruments to evaluate structural, clinical, and recovery factors influencing case outcomes. Each instrument produces a separate analytical report.

Select any instrument below to learn more.

CCI Case Complexity Index
+
Evaluates injury severity, life disruption, and care coordination demands throughout the case lifecycle.
CS Aud. Clinical Sequencing Auditor
+
Assesses alignment with the 7 Rights of Injury Care Management and treatment pathway defensibility.
CAF C.A.R.E. Access Friction Score
+
Identifies insurance, referral, and provider access barriers that may delay appropriate care.
HSI Holistic Stability Index
+
Evaluates stability across the 4Ps of Wellness and social determinants of health.
FCI Functional Capacity Index
+
Assesses capacity to return to work and maintain independence following injury.
RRF Recovery Risk Forecaster
+
Identifies psychological, behavioral, and situational factors affecting recovery trajectory.

Each tool may be used independently. Together, they provide a comprehensive view of the factors influencing case outcomes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions
from attorneys.

1. What is the rationale for offering this at no cost? +
We believe the value of these tools is best demonstrated through direct application to real cases. Rather than describing what the assessment reveals, we invite you to experience it. The complimentary assessment is a demonstration of capability — not a sales tactic.
2. Which cases are most suitable for assessment? +
The forensic tool suite was developed specifically for personal injury, workers' compensation, and disability litigation. The tools are most effective for cases involving physical injury, chronic condition, or functional limitation.
3. What information will I need to provide? +
Basic case demographics, injury type and date, current treatment status, and relevant medical history. No confidential client records are required at this stage. Specific information gathering occurs through a secure portal after your request is received.
4. Will I be contacted after receiving the reports? +
The reports are yours to retain and use as you see fit. There is no obligation to engage further services. If you choose to continue the conversation, we are available to discuss findings or explore ongoing case management support.
5. Are the tools applicable to both plaintiff and defense work? +
Standard medical record review documents what occurred. These tools evaluate whether what occurred was clinically appropriate, whether care pathways were defensible, and what barriers or risk factors may have influenced the outcome. They provide a different class of insight — one oriented toward case strategy rather than documentation.

Before Requesting Access

To ensure the assessment provides meaningful results, participating attorneys should be prepared to provide structured case information including:

—injury type and severity
—treatment progression
—provider involvement
—functional recovery factors
—barriers affecting care coordination

Most attorneys complete the case information portion of the assessment over multiple sessions during the assessment window.

Each instrument typically requires approximately 10–15 minutes to complete, and the full assessment generally requires approximately 60–90 minutes.

Because the tools require thoughtful case evaluation, the portal remains open for 72 hours from first login.

Participation Notice

Participation in the Case Clarity Project is intended for attorneys handling matters involving complex medical treatment, delayed recovery, or uncertain case outcomes.

Participants are asked to identify one active case and one previously closed case when submitting their request.

If the submitted cases appear appropriate for the assessment, access credentials for the assessment portal will be provided by email, allowing you to begin the evaluation process.

Most requests are reviewed and approved within one business day.

Case Selection Guidance

To ensure the assessment provides meaningful insight, we recommend selecting:

— one active case where additional clinical or strategic insight would be valuable
— one previously closed case that presented unexpected challenges or outcomes

These cases allow the instruments to evaluate both current case dynamics and retrospective case factors.
