How Cases Are Counted

Active, Closed & Dormant — Defined

Your tier's case capacity refers specifically to active cases. Understanding the distinction between case states is essential for selecting the right tier.

Active

Active Case

An active case occupies one slot in your tier's concurrent capacity. All six tools are available and unlimited reports can be generated at any point.

  • Counts toward your tier's capacity limit
  • All tools accessible with no report limits
  • Can be reassessed at any treatment milestone
  • Moves to Closed or Dormant when appropriate
Pricing is based on active case slots — not on report volume.
Closed

Closed Case

A closed case has reached its end state — settlement, resolution, or formal discharge. Closed cases release their slot back to your active capacity.

  • No longer occupies a capacity slot
  • Prior assessments and reports remain accessible
  • Cannot receive new assessments once closed
  • Can be reopened if case status changes
Closing a case frees your slot for a new active case.
Dormant

Dormant Case

A dormant case is temporarily paused — treatment on hold, client non-responsive, or file pending an external event.

  • Still occupies a capacity slot while dormant
  • Can be reactivated without data loss
  • No new assessments during dormancy
  • Intended for temporary holds, not closure
Dormant cases count against your active limit until formally closed.
