Case Complexity Index™

Understanding Case Severity, Recovery Disruption, and Coordination Demands

The Case Complexity Index™ is a clinical assessment tool designed to help attorneys quickly understand the true complexity of an injury case. By evaluating injury severity, functional system disruption, and care coordination demands, the index provides a structured complexity level that helps guide case management strategy, recovery oversight, and litigation planning.

Why This Tool Matters

Many injury cases appear straightforward at first glance but later develop complications that significantly affect recovery timelines, treatment coordination, and case value.

The Case Complexity Index helps identify those risks early by analyzing three core dimensions:

  • Injury Severity
  • Functional System Disruption (4Ps of Wellness)
  • Medical & Care Coordination Demands

Together, these factors create a clearer picture of the case trajectory and potential recovery challenges.

What the Assessment Evaluates

The Case Complexity Index examines the following three domains:

Injury Severity

The physiological seriousness of the injury, including catastrophic indicators such as neurologic compromise, organ failure, ventilator dependence, or major trauma.

Injury severity carries the greatest weight in the overall complexity determination.

Functional System Disruption (4Ps)

The extent to which the injury disrupts key life domains:

  • Physical function
  • Psychological stability
  • Psychosocial stability
  • Professional and economic functioning

These disruptions often determine whether recovery proceeds smoothly or becomes unstable.

Care Coordination Demands

The number of medical specialties involved, referral pathways, treatment barriers, and pre-existing medical conditions that may complicate recovery.

Cases with higher coordination demands often require structured oversight to prevent delays or fragmentation of care.

Important Note Before Beginning

This assessment is only as accurate as the information entered. The tool should not be completed quickly or with partial information.

For best results:

  • Review available medical records
  • Confirm major injuries and complications
  • Consider both injury-related and pre-existing conditions
  • Identify functional impacts across life domains

Most assessments take approximately 5–10 minutes to complete.

Confidentiality

This tool does not store or transmit case data.

All information entered into the assessment remains within the user's session. Only the user sees the results.
