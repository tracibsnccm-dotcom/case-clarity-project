Before You Begin — Case Complexity Index™

A structured read on injury severity, functional disruption (4Ps), and care coordination demands. The report supports strategy and oversight—not a substitute for medical judgment.

The Case Complexity Index™ helps attorneys see how injury severity, multi-domain functional impact, and coordination load interact—so complications that affect timelines, treatment, and value are visible earlier.

  • Answer from the case environment as it stands today.
  • Take enough time to be accurate; rushed input skews the picture.
  • Three core dimensions: injury severity, functional system disruption (4Ps), and medical & care coordination demands.

Injury severity carries the greatest weight in the overall complexity level. The index blends all three domains into one structured complexity level.

  • 1. Injury severity — Physiologic seriousness, including catastrophic indicators (e.g., major neurologic compromise, organ failure, ventilator dependence, severe trauma).
  • 2. Functional disruption (4Ps) — Physical, psychological, psychosocial, and professional/economic impacts that shape whether recovery stays stable.
  • 3. Care coordination — Specialties, referrals, barriers, and pre-existing conditions that raise oversight and fragmentation risk.

Output quality depends on complete, accurate input—avoid rushing or omitting material facts.

Before you begin

Review records where available; confirm major injuries and complications; weigh injury-related and pre-existing factors; note functional impacts across life domains.

Typical completion time: approximately 5–10 minutes.

This tool does not store or transmit case data.

All information entered into the assessment remains within the user's session. Only the user sees the results.

The level-based review interval provides the baseline monitoring cadence for this tool. The triggers below indicate circumstances that may justify an off-cycle re-run before the next routine review point.

Re-run this assessment when:

  • Additional medical records are received that document new injuries, complications, or catastrophic indicators
  • A new specialty physician is added or the referral chain expands
  • Functional status changes meaningfully across any of the 4Ps domains
  • Pre-existing conditions are newly disclosed, clarified, or identified in the record
  • Treatment is denied, delayed, interrupted, or materially altered by the payer
  • The case transitions between acute, rehabilitative, and long-term management phases
  • A new catastrophic event occurs, including re-hospitalization, surgical complication, or new neurologic finding
